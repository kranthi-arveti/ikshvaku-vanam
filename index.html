About the Farmhouse

A grove built around stone, water and open sky

Ikshvaku Vanam sits on a quiet stretch of land in Rachloor village, on the outskirts of Hyderabad. The farmhouse is built low into the earth, wrapped in rugged natural stone, with a sunken courtyard where a cluster of boulders and a still reflecting pool anchor the space between the house and the sky above.

Wide verandas, wood-lined eaves and sliding glass fronts keep every room connected to the outdoors — the lawns, the trees planted along the boundary, and the long, unhurried evenings that a working farmhouse in the countryside is meant for.

It's a place for weekend getaways, family get-togethers, and small celebrations away from the noise of the city — with the comforts of a proper home.
